The History of Giant Pandas at the Smithsonian's National Zoo and Conservation Biology Institute

Ling-Ling and Hsing-Hsing, the Zoo's first giant pandas

On April 16, 1972, the giant pandas Ling-Ling (a female) and Hsing-Hsing (a male) arrived at their new home.

1992

Ling-Ling died Dec. 30.

1999

Hsing-Hsing, suffering from kidney disease, was euthanized Nov. 28, 1999.

The next chapter: Tian Tian, Mei Xiang, and advances in conservation technology

On Dec. 6, 2000, giant pandas Mei Xiang (female) and Tian Tian (male) came to live at the Zoo.

On July 9, 2005, giant panda Mei Xiang gave birth to Tai Shan, the first surviving giant panda cub born at the Smithsonian's National Zoo.

2010

Tai Shan, the only surviving giant panda cub born at the Zoo, left for Wolong Nature Reserve, China, Feb. 4, to participate in breeding research.

2013

Mei Xiang gave birth to Bao Bao Aug. 23.

Bao Bao was sired by Tian Tian.

2015

Giant panda Bei Bei was born Aug. 22, 2015, at the Zoo’s David M. Rubenstein Family Giant Panda Habitat.

2017

Mei Xiang’s second surviving cub, Bao Bao, departed for China on Feb. 21, 2017.

2019

Mei Xiang’s third surviving cub, Bei Bei, departed for China on Nov. 19, 2019.

2020

Mei Xiang was artificially inseminated using frozen semen from Tian Tian and gave birth to a cub on Aug. 21. The cub is male. On Nov. 23, the cub was named Xiao Qi Ji.

2023

On Nov. 8, 2023, Tian Tian, Mei Xiang and 3-year-old cub Xiao Qi Ji (male) departed from the Smithsonian's National Zoo and Conservation Biology Institute.

Welcoming the new panda pair: Bao Li and Qing Bao

The Chinese government agreed to send two young giant pandas, Qing Bao (female) and Bao Li (male) to the Zoo. The pair made their official public debut at the David M. Rubenstein Family Giant Panda Exhibit on Jan. 24, 2025.

2024

After months of preparation, giant pandas Bao Li and Qing Bao arrived at the Smithsonian’s National Zoo and Conservation Biology Institute on Oct. 15, 2024.
