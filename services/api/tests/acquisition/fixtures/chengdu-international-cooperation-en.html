International Cooperation

Breeding Cooperation

Adventure World in Shirahama, Wakayama, Japan

Since 1994, the Panda Base and Adventure World have carried on an international cooperative breeding program. Giant panda Mei Mei gave birth to 9 cubs in 5 births in 2000, 2001, 2003, 2005, and 2006 respectively.

Zoo Atlanta in the United States

The giant panda Lun Lun living in the United States delivered a total of 7 cubs in 5 births in 2006, 2008, 2010, and 2013.

Among those born babies, Mei Lan born in 2006 has returned to China.

Madrid Zoo in Spain

The female giant panda Hua Zui Ba successfully gave birth to 6 cubs in 4 births in 2010, 2013, 2016, and 2021.

Beauval Zoo in France

On January 15, 2012, the male giant panda Yuan Zai and the female giant panda Huan Huan at the Base flew from Chengdu to Beauval Zoo in France by a special plane.

On July 25, 2023, Yuan Meng, the first giant panda born in France, began his journey back to China.

Berlin Zoo in Germany

The two giant pandas Meng Meng and Jiao Qing selected from the Panda Base arrived in Germany on June 24, 2017, and moved into the Panda House of Berlin Zoo in Germany on July 5 of the same year. On September 1, 2019, Meng Meng successfully gave birth to two cubs.

Copenhagen Zoo in Denmark

In 2019, the Sino-Danish International Giant Panda Breeding Cooperation Plan was officially launched. On April 4 of the same year, giant pandas Xing Er and Mao Er successfully flew from Chengdu to Copenhagen Zoo in Denmark by a special plane.
