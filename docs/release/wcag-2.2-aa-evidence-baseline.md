# WCAG 2.2 Level AA launch evidence baseline

This baseline defines the minimum evidence for a defensible Panda Atlas launch decision. It is an evidence policy, not a conformance claim by itself.

## Decision rule and scope

The launch scope is the complete public Chinese and English journeys, including every responsive variation and every state exposed while completing them:

1. enter the home/atlas experience, navigate, search, filter, and inspect results;
2. open a panda profile and use its actions;
3. explore distribution information through the map and its text equivalent;
4. explore lineage relationships through the lineage view and its text equivalent;
5. switch between Chinese and English and complete the same journeys.

A **WCAG 2.2 Level AA: Go** decision requires evidence that every applicable Level A and AA success criterion passes for the full pages and complete processes in scope. A component, map canvas, drawer, transient state, or responsive presentation cannot be excluded. Any unresolved A/AA failure makes the decision **No-Go for an AA claim**; a product-risk acceptance is not a WCAG conformance waiver. This follows WCAG's Level AA, full-page, complete-process, accessibility-supported-use, and non-interference requirements: [WCAG 2.2, Conformance Requirements](https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG22/#conformance-reqs).

Use the WCAG-EM sequence to record the scope, technologies, representative pages and states, evaluation results, and findings. Include all pages/states when feasible; otherwise include a structured sample covering key functionality, templates, content types, responsive variants, and complete processes, plus a random sample: [WCAG-EM 1.0](https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG-EM/) and [WCAG-EM overview](https://www.w3.org/WAI/test-evaluate/conformance/wcag-em/).

The evidence bundle must identify the release commit/artifact, date, environment, browser/OS/assistive-technology versions, locale, viewport/zoom, journey and UI state, evaluator, method, result by success criterion, defect link, and retained screenshot/video/log where appropriate. Accessibility support is environment- and version-specific, including the browser/AT combination and known limitations: [Understanding Accessibility Support](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/documenting-accessibility-support).

## What automation can and cannot establish

Automated Playwright/axe checks can reproducibly detect some machine-testable failures, such as invalid or missing accessible names/properties, some contrast failures, invalid language metadata, duplicate IDs, and selected structural rules. Run scans after each relevant dynamic state is rendered, and retain the complete results, including `incomplete` items. Passing scans prove only that the configured rules found no violations in the rendered states tested. Playwright explicitly states that automated tests cannot detect every WCAG violation and recommends automated, manual, and inclusive user testing together: [Playwright accessibility testing](https://playwright.dev/docs/accessibility-testing). WAI likewise states that tools cannot determine accessibility and human judgement is required: [Selecting Web Accessibility Evaluation Tools](https://www.w3.org/WAI/test-evaluate/tools/selecting/).

Automation can also replay a specified keyboard path, assert the active element, measure target geometry, inspect DOM order, validate `lang` attributes, emulate `prefers-reduced-motion`, and capture screenshots. Those checks prove the encoded assertions and prevent regressions; they do not prove that the focus or reading order is meaningful, that Chinese or English is pronounced correctly, that a map/lineage alternative conveys equivalent information and purpose, or that an interaction is understandable with assistive technology.

> **Human screen-reader evidence is mandatory. An AI agent, browser accessibility-tree snapshot, DOM/ARIA inspection, or scripted key sequence cannot stand in for a human operating and listening to a real screen reader.** The signed result must name the human evaluator, their language proficiency, the real browser/AT/OS/version combination, the journey and states exercised, and observed announcements or failures. WAI says knowledgeable human evaluation is required and effective evaluation needs assistive-technology and disability-barrier expertise: [Evaluating Web Accessibility](https://www.w3.org/WAI/test-evaluate/) and [Using Combined Expertise](https://www.w3.org/WAI/test-evaluate/combined-expertise/).

## Practical evidence matrix

| Evidence area | Automated evidence accepted | Required human evidence | Launch record / pass condition |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Scope and coverage | Route/state inventory tied to the release; Playwright coverage for every listed journey, locale, breakpoint, empty/loading/error/drawer state | Evaluator confirms the inventory contains all full pages, responsive variations, and complete processes | Signed inventory and WCAG-EM report; no unexplained gaps. Sampling must follow [WCAG-EM](https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG-EM/). |
| Semantic structure and names | axe results; headings/landmarks; accessible-name, role, value, relationship and status-message assertions | Screen-reader navigation by landmarks/headings/controls; confirm labels, expanded/selected/current state, result counts, loading, empty and error announcements are understandable | Zero unresolved A/AA failures. Dynamic messages that do not move focus must be exposed as required by [SC 4.1.3](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/status-messages). |
| Keyboard, focus, and controls | Scripted `Tab`/`Shift+Tab`, activation, Escape and arrow-key paths; active-element and no-trap assertions; target-size geometry | Keyboard-only completion of every journey: logical order, visible persistent focus, no hidden focus, no trap, predictable focus after navigation/drawers/filters, and workable composite widgets | Human pass plus recordings/notes. Apply [SC 2.1.1](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/keyboard), [2.1.2](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/no-keyboard-trap), [2.4.3](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/focus-order), [2.4.7](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/focus-visible), [2.4.11](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/focus-not-obscured-minimum), [2.5.7](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/dragging-movements), and [2.5.8](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/target-size-minimum). For custom composites, compare behavior with the non-normative [ARIA APG keyboard guidance](https://www.w3.org/WAI/ARIA/apg/practices/keyboard-interface/); ARIA alone does not add keyboard behavior. |
| Human screen reader, Chinese | Automation may validate `lang="zh-CN"`, roles, names, and live-region state | A Chinese-proficient human completes all journeys with a real supported browser/screen-reader pair; verifies pronunciation, control/state announcements, reading order, focus recovery, dynamic updates, map facts, and lineage facts | Signed session notes with versions and observed output; no blocking loss of meaning or operation. |
| Human screen reader, English | Automation may validate `lang="en"`, roles, names, and live-region state | An English-proficient human repeats the complete journeys with a real supported browser/screen-reader pair and the same checks | Signed session notes with versions and observed output; no blocking loss of meaning or operation. Browser/AT pairs must be declared rather than assumed; see [accessibility support documentation](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/documenting-accessibility-support). |
| Language and reading order | Validate page/part `lang`; compare DOM order snapshots; flag visual/DOM reordering | In each locale, read linearly and by screen reader; verify mixed-language panda/institution names are marked where required, pronunciation is usable, and visual, DOM, and focus sequences preserve meaning | Pass [SC 1.3.2](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/meaningful-sequence), [3.1.1](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/language-of-page), and [3.1.2](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/language-of-parts). A valid `lang` token alone is not evidence of correct pronunciation or reading order. |
| 200% text resize and reflow | Screenshot/diff and overflow/clipping assertions at 200% zoom; viewport test at 320 CSS px; exercise all interactive states | Human checks both locales for clipped/overlapped text, lost controls/information, unexpected two-dimensional page scrolling, readable dialogs/drawers, and reachable focus | No loss at 200% under [SC 1.4.4](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/resize-text); no loss or two-dimensional scrolling at 320 CSS px except content that genuinely requires two dimensions under [SC 1.4.10](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/reflow). The 320 CSS px test is distinct from the 200% text-resize test (W3C notes it corresponds to 400% zoom from 1280 CSS px). |
| Contrast, colour, text spacing, and hover/focus content | axe/static computed-colour checks; text-spacing injection; screenshots for default, hover, focus, selected, disabled, and map-overlay states | Visual review of gradients, imagery/canvas overlays, focus indicators, colour-independent meaning, text-spacing loss, and dismissible/hoverable/persistent popovers | All applicable [Distinguishable A/AA criteria](https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG22/#distinguishable) pass; all axe `incomplete` results are resolved by a person. |
| Reduced motion and moving content | With OS/media preference emulation, assert non-essential transitions stop; assert automatically moving content can be paused/stopped/hidden; scan for flashing risks | Motion-sensitive review of map movement, drawer/page transitions, loading effects, hover/focus animation, and unexpected movement in both locales | Pass AA-relevant [SC 2.2.2](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/pause-stop-hide) and [2.3.1](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/three-flashes-or-below-threshold). Respecting reduced motion for interaction-triggered animation is a Panda Atlas launch requirement and strong practice, but [SC 2.3.3](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/animation-from-interactions) is Level AAA and must not be misreported as an AA requirement. |
| Distribution map equivalent | Assert a named map region/control set, keyboard/single-pointer alternatives, and a reachable synchronized textual location/result representation | Without seeing or manipulating the map, a human verifies the text path exposes the same decision-relevant panda/location facts, filters, selection, detail navigation, current state, and updates; keyboard and screen-reader users complete the journey | The map's two-dimensional-layout exception in [SC 1.4.10](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/reflow) does not exempt surrounding controls or equivalent information/functionality. The text alternative must serve the equivalent purpose under [SC 1.1.1](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/non-text-content), and all functionality remains keyboard operable. |
| Lineage equivalent | Assert an accessible named structure/list/table, parent/child relation text, expansion state, and detail links; compare entity/relation IDs with the visual graph data | A human linearizes the lineage and verifies identity, relationship direction, generations, unknown/uncertain states, selection, expansion and detail navigation are understandable without the graph | No fact/action available only by position, connector, colour, hover, or drag. Complex visual information requires a brief identification plus an equivalent long text presentation when a short alternative is insufficient: [Understanding SC 1.1.1](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/non-text-content). |

## Required sign-off packet

The release owner may record **Go** only when the evidence bundle contains:

- the immutable release identifier and exact scope/state inventory;
- full automated results for both locales, with every violation and `incomplete` item resolved;
- human keyboard, 200% resize, 320 CSS px reflow, text-spacing, contrast/colour, focus, and reduced-motion records;
- separate signed Chinese and English real-screen-reader records, including browser/AT/OS versions;
- human semantic-equivalence records for map and lineage text paths;
- a criterion-by-criterion A/AA report with zero unresolved failures and a list of non-blocking best-practice/AAA observations kept distinct from conformance;
- reviewer and release-owner signatures, date, defects and retest evidence.

User testing improves evaluation quality but does not replace criterion-by-criterion conformance evaluation, and conformance review does not replace testing with users with disabilities: [Involving Users in Evaluating Web Accessibility](https://www.w3.org/WAI/test-evaluate/involving-users/). Retain both kinds of evidence when available.
